# 5 Signs Your Bike Needs Servicing NOW

Your bike is pretty good at telling you when something's wrong—you just have to know what to listen for. Here are five clear signs that it's time to book a service.

## 1. Strange Noises

Not all bike noises are normal. Here's how to tell the difference:

**Normal sounds:**
- Chain whirring
- tyre hiss on pavement
- Subtle clicking while pedalling (often just the chain settling)

**Sounds that mean trouble:**
- **Grinding** from the bottom bracket = worn bearings
- **Squeaking** from brakes = pads need adjusting or replacing
- **Clunking** when pedalling = loose crank arm or chain
- **Rubbing** sound = brake rotor touching pad

If your bike is making sounds it shouldn't, bring it in.

## 2. Gears Not Shifting Properly

Gear issues are one of the most common problems we see, and they're usually caused by:
- Stretched chain
- Worn derailleur cables
- Misaligned derailleur
- Dirty cables

If your gears are skipping, jumping, or won't shift into certain cogs, it's a quick fix—but only if you address it early. A neglected drivetrain will eat through replacement parts fast.

## 3. Brakes Feel Wrong

Your brakes are literally the most important part of your bike. Get them checked if:
- The lever pulls all the way to the handlebar (low on fluid or cable stretched)
- You have to pull hard to stop (pads worn)
- The brake feels spongy (air in hydraulic line)
- There's a grinding sound (pads worn to metal)

**Pro tip:** If your bike has cable disc brakes, check the cable tension monthly. It's the most common cause of poor braking.

## 4. Wobbling Wheels

Wheel issues can be dangerous. Visit us if:
- Your wheel wobbles side-to-side when spinning (wheel out of true)
- The wheel doesn't spin freely (cone adjustment needed)
- You feel "soft" when riding over bumps (possible loose hub bearings)
- Spokes feel uneven (some may be broken or loose)

We can true wheels, replace broken spokes, and service hubs. Usually much cheaper than a new wheel.

## 5. Rust and Corrosion

A little surface rust on the chain is normal—but anything more is a problem:
- **Chain rust:** The links will stiffen and skip. Replace it.
- **Frame rust:** Check around cable entry points and where paint has chipped. Surface rust can be treated; deep rust is serious.
- **Bolt rust:** If bolts are rusty, they may snap when you try to remove them. Best to replace.

## The Cheapest Time to Service

Small problems become expensive problems. A £25 gear adjustment today might become a £150 drivetrain replacement in six months if ignored.

**Book your service early. Your bike—and your wallet—will thank you.**
