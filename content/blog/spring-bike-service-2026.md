# Spring Bike Service: The Complete Guide to Waking Up Your Ride

After months of winter storage, your bike is probably not in the best shape. Cold temperatures, indoor storage, and simply sitting idle can take a real toll on your machine. That's where we come in.

## Why Spring Service Matters

Think about it: your bike has been sitting in a garage, shed, or possibly crumpled in a corner of your living room. The rubber has lost elasticity, the oil has gone stale, and who knows what else has happened to it. A proper spring service isn't just about making your bike look good—it's about keeping you safe.

## Our 12-Point Spring Inspection

When you bring your bike to us, here's what we check:

### 1. Frame & Fork Inspection
We look for any cracks, dents, or damage that might have occurred over winter. The frame is the backbone of your bike—literally—and any issues need to be caught early.

### 2. Brakes
This is the most critical safety check. We inspect:
- Brake pads for wear (replace if below 3mm)
- Cable tension and housing condition
- Brake lever feel and positioning
- Disc brake rotor condition (warped, worn, or contaminated)

### 3. Gears & Drivetrain
Your drivetrain has likely accumulated grime and lost some performance:
- Chain inspection (stretch, wear, rust)
- Cassette condition
- Derailleur alignment and tension
- Cable housing condition
- Shifting performance across all gears

### 4. Wheels & Tyres
- Spoke tension and true
- Hub bearings (play or roughness)
- Tyre condition (cracks, wear, punctures)
- Tyre pressure (should be checked regularly anyway!)

### 5. Bottom Bracket
The heart of your pedalling efficiency:
- Check for play or roughness
- Lubrication status

### 6. Headset
A often-overlooked component:
- Check for roughness when turning
- Adjust if there's play

### 7. Saddle & Seatpost
- Check saddle for damage
- Ensure seatpost isn't seized (common issue!)

### 8. Handlebars & Stem
- Check for cracks or damage
- Ensure stem bolts are properly torqued
- Bar tape/grips condition

### 9. Cables & Housing
Winter can be brutal on cables:
- Inspect for fraying
- Check housing for cracks
- Test all cable-operated components

### 10. Lighting & Electronics
- Test front and rear lights
- Check dynamo hub function if applicable
- Test any integrated lights

### 11. Bolts & Fasteners
We go through and ensure everything is properly torqued:
- Pedals
- Stem bolts
- Seatpost clamp
- Bottle cage bolts
- Wheel axle nuts/quick releases

### 12. Clean & Lubricate
Finally, we give your bike a proper clean and relube:
- Degrease drivetrain
- Clean frame
- Re-lube chain
- Apply lubricant to cables where needed

## Common Spring Problems We See

### The "Seized" Seatpost
One of the most common issues. If your bike was stored in a damp environment, the seatpost can corrode to the frame. We have special tools to extract stuck seatposts without damaging your frame.

### Cable Issues
Cables stretch over time, and housing cracks in the cold. If your gears or brakes feel sluggish, it's often a cable issue.

### Tyre Damage
Tyres develop flat spots from being stored in one position. Inspect for cracks on the sidewalls—even if the tyres look fine, the rubber may have degraded.

### Chain Stretch
A stretched chain wears out your cassette and chainrings quickly. We measure chain stretch and recommend replacement if needed.
