# Why E-Bikes Are Breaking Bike Shops (And What to Do About It)

*The e-bike revolution isn't coming—it's already here. And most shops aren't ready.*

---

If you run a bike shop, you've probably noticed the change. The customer walks in with an e-bike that looks like a normal bike but costs £3,000. They want a service. You quote them an hour. Three hours later, you're still figuring out the battery system.

This is the new reality of the bike industry. E-bikes are fundamentally different from traditional bicycles—and most shops are completely unprepared to handle them.

## The E-Bike Problem

Here's what every shop owner needs to understand:

### 1. Repair Time Has Tripled

A traditional bike service: 45-60 minutes.
An e-bike service: 2-4 hours.

Why? Because e-bikes have:
- Complex battery management systems
- Torque sensors that require precise calibration
- Integrated wiring that's different on every brand
- Proprietary tools required for major brands
- Software/firmware that needs updating

### 2. The Parts Situation Is a Nightmare

Traditional bike: Any generic brake pad fits.
E-bike: You need the exact part number from the exact manufacturer.

Many shops are keeping thousands of pounds in e-bike parts inventory—money they never had to spend before.

### 3. Liability Is Through the Roof

E-bikes can reach speeds of 28mph (class 3). If something goes wrong—brakes fail, battery malfunctions—who's liable? The shop that touched it last.

Most shop insurance doesn't cover e-bikes properly. This is a ticking time bomb.

### 4. The Skills Gap Is Real

Your best mechanic knows how to work on traditional bikes. But e-bikes require different training, different tools, and different thinking.

The average mechanic? They're learning on the job. Mistakes are expensive.

---

## What Successful Shops Are Doing

We've talked to dozens of shops handling the e-bike boom successfully. Here's what works:

### 1. Specialize or Partner

Some shops are becoming e-bike specialists. They focus exclusively on e-bikes and charge premium prices. Others are partnering with specialist e-bike service centers for referrals.

### 2. Invest in Training

Bosch, Shimano, and other major e-bike systems all offer certification programs. The shops investing in training are the ones getting the repeat customers.

### 3. Use Digital Tools to Track Complex Jobs

E-bike jobs aren't like regular services. They need:
- Battery health checks
- Software version tracking
- Multiple diagnostic passes
- Special parts ordering workflows

Shops using digital job management are handling 2x the volume with half the mistakes.

### 4. Set Clear Expectations

The shops doing best with e-bikes? They educate customers upfront. "This will take longer because it's an e-bike. Here's why the price is different."

Here's the thing most shop owners miss: E-bikes aren't going away. They're the biggest growth area in cycling.

The shops that figure this out? They're going to dominate their local markets for the next decade.

But it requires change. New skills. New tools. New workflows.
